What a concerned Nigerian student in Noida tweeted to Sushma Swaraj


Updated: Mar 28, 2017, 10.48 AM IST

The death by drug overdose of a Class 12 student sparked protests in Noida Residents alleged it was "Nigerians" living in the area who supplied the drugs to the young student. There have been a series of attacks on African students around the country in recent years NEW DELHI: A day after four African students were badly beaten up in Noida, a Nigerian student tweeted to Union minister Sushma Swaraj that "living for us in Noida is becoming a life threatening issue."

The external affairs minister, who spoke to Uttar Pradesh chief minister Adityanath this morning about the incident, assured the student, Sadiq Bello, that the government "will take immediate action." Yesterday's was only the latest in a series attacks on Africans and African students, in Delhi, Bengaluru and some other parts of the country. Yesterday evening, the foreign students sustained severe injuries after being attacked by a group of local residents in Greater Noida's Pari Chowk area in Uttar Pradesh.

This morning, Swaraj tweeted that the Uttar Pradesh chief minister Adityanath assured her a "fair and impartial investigation." Yesterday, the death by drug overdose of a Class 12 student sparked protests in Greater Noida's Pari Chowk area in Uttar Pradesh, which led to the beating of the foreign students.

In keeping with the stereotype that "Nigerians sell drugs", police arrested five Nigerian students and later released them for lack of evidence. After they were released, the residents of the area where the Class 12 student lived, beat up four of them, saying the "Nigerians" were responsible for the Class 12 student's death. They also demanded all Africans living in residential colonies in Greater Noida be asked to vacate their rented houses immediately.
